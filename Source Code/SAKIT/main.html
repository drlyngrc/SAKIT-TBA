Plant Disease

Bacterial spot is a plant disease that affects many crops. It is caused by bacteria such as Xylella fastidiosa and Xanthomonas vesicatoria, among others. The disease can cause spots on the foliage, fruit, or stems of the plant, which can be yellow, brown, or black.

Common rust is a fungal disease that is often found in Midwest cornfields during the growing season. Common rust can decrease yield by increasing the potential for poorly filled kernels, as well as stalk rot and lodging. Preventing this disease with a timely fungicide application can protect corn yield.

Common rust

Common rust

Early blight is a common tomato disease caused by the fungus Alternaria solani or closely related Alternaria tomatophila. This disease should not be confused with late blight, which occurs later in the growing season, is much more deadly, and can spread much further and more quickly.
